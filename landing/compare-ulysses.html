Cafezin vs Ulysses

Ulysses is a beautifully crafted distraction-free writing app with great iCloud sync and export. Cafezin is a local-first AI writing workspace with reviewable AI edits, canvas planning, and git — designed for writers who want more AI control and full file ownership.

Updated: March 2026

Short answer: Choose Ulysses for a polished distraction-free long-form writing experience with great Apple sync. Choose Cafezin when you want AI writing assistance, visual planning, and local Markdown files that belong to you and only you.

Where Cafezin is stronger

  • Integrated AI writing assistance with reviewable edit tracking
  • Canvas for visual planning alongside documents
  • Files stored as plain Markdown — no proprietary format
  • Built-in git for free and transparent version history
  • No iCloud or subscription lock-in for file access
  • Voice capture for hands-free idea recording

Where Ulysses is stronger

  • Polished, distraction-free writing environment
  • Seamless iCloud sync across Mac, iPhone, iPad
  • Export to EPUB, PDF, Word, and publishing platforms
  • Goal tracking features for word count and progress
  • Well-established for novelists and long-form bloggers

The real difference

Ulysses stores content in its own library format backed by iCloud. You get a smooth experience but trade some file portability. Cafezin writes plain Markdown files to disk — you own them, git-track them, and can open them in any editor. The AI assistance in Cafezin is also more integrated and transparent than bolt-on AI in Ulysses.

Who should choose what

Choose Cafezin if you want AI help throughout your writing process, visual planning, and files that are fully portable. Choose Ulysses if you want a highly polished environment with Apple-first sync and strong export options but don't need deep AI integration.
